Somebody near you is one player short.

The whole app exists to close that gap. Two activities, one city, one job: get a real person onto a real court on a real Friday. Everything below is drawn for Wigston, LE18 — the actual venues, the actual distances, the actual £20 court split four ways.

Getting in

Two questions, then you're through. We ask what you play and roughly where you live, because those are the only two things the app needs to know to be useful. No bio, no photo, no profile to "complete".

The loop that matters

Somebody's short a player → everyone nearby who plays that thing gets told → one of them taps I'm in → the court gets booked and the money gets split. That's Hangout. Tap the button on the Friday game below and watch it happen.

The people

The original problem was never "no pitches". It was "nobody to ask". So there's a screen whose only job is to prove that someone near you plays your game — and a way to say hello that isn't weird.

Deliberately not in v1

Everything you described is in here — it's just queued behind the one thing that has to work first.

  • Wallet & split paymentsHolding money in the UK means FCA permissions and KYC. v1 shows "£5.00 each" and links out; a real wallet arrives once people are actually playing.
  • How-to-play videosThe embedded YouTube guides and likes. Genuinely good for beginners like Meera — but it retains people who already came, it doesn't bring them.
  • Book club, library, parent & toddlerSame app, different chip. Turning them on is a config change once cricket and badminton have proved the loop in Leicester.
  • Communities & groupsRight now a "community" is just the people on a recurring game. Formal groups earn their place when a game outgrows its four players.
  • In-app bookingWe'll always link out to Active Wigston and the rest. Becoming a booking engine is a different, much worse company.
